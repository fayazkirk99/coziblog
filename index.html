11 MAY 2026

I had just moved from Lilongwe to Blantyre to live with my grandmother and proceed in my ambitious pursuit of formal education as a computing student. It was about 9 hours and possibly 30 or maybe 45 minutes into a Wednesday morning when I was pacing through the streets of Blantyre's town. My forehead was glistening from sweat, not due to the excessive amount of lotion I applied on my brown face earlier in my attempt to keep up with the day's schedule. Instead, the culprit responsible was the relentless blazing sun that came out to greet each Malawian on his way to adhere to our incompetent capitalistic economy. My hair was being kept steady at the back of my head by these black affordable headphones, holding my ears' hostage as the 808s blasted modern trap music at the highest possible volume, but instead of my ears bleeding my body was receiving excessive amounts of energy that kept me pacing. I had taken a countable amount of steps, passing the bus stop aiming for the school campus, when I noticed a group of girls turning the corner while chatting, exchanging laughs with every 2 steps they each took.I could also notice that they had a schedule to attend to as well. One of the girls was dressed in muslim attire and within that short moment my eyes could not help but to throw a tiny gaze towards her inviting face. A tiny gaze that turned out to seem infinite because within those few seconds, she was the most beautiful girl in the world. My mind is sort of convinced that she exchanged a gaze with me, but I half believed it could be my ego luring my brain into a pool of delusion. There's something peculiar about seeing something beautiful, whether it be a woman or other majestic objects of nature that just, in the simplest of words, keeps a guy like me going. I did not dwell in the moment for too long in the present because class was where I ought to be. I quickly shuffled through the streets walking past vendors in the same perimeter. Each vendor had in front of them a layout of familiar products you could get from the next vendor for a similar if not identical price. I grabbed a piece of candy to sweeten the taste of my breath while listening to what seemed to be the final song to escape my headphones and into my ears before I reached the main door to my supposed class. I was the first one to be present, so much for early birds and catching worms.

08 MAY 2026

There is nothing quite like sharing a smoke with a few friends after a long day of chasing college schedules, and adhering to their related authorities.The schoolgrounds are not as exciting in this small country of ours,or at least not as exciting as the western college films that i grew up watching as a kid.Films like American Pie where the producers tend to put more emphasis on the fun and drunken lifestyle the youth in college seem to rejoice in.As expected those films were as far as the stars when it came to being a reality.Of course there's some youth around here that probably have the perception that they are having the time of their lives but that is due to the mediocrity that stems from our society's lack of ambition.I'm at the spot with the guys, usually we have my classmate here with us but he's absent due to his parents leaving the house to himself for the weekend.I’d spoken to him earlier,he sounded like he’d finally found some personal space, a solitude I didn't want to disturb.Locked in, a few of the guys eyes were glued to their smartphone screens in a horizontal stance firing at the opposition, because mobile games are sort of like therapy to these guys here.I on the other hand had never been one with much interest in mobile games except when GTA san andreas was the hottest thing in my preteen phase.Since back then i havent had another sip from that cup of tea.It was already dark out,the moon fully beaming and i imagined it still shinning with the same vigor it had on its first day of creation.One of the guys finally grabbed a lighter, prepared to inhale as we let the swift spark conclude our day.
